Homemade Birria Burrito

Authentic birria is some of the most flavorful food in the world. It is an authentic Mexican dish that can be eaten as a taco, a burrito or even by itself!


Surprisingly, this slow cooked dish is very easy to make and will take your mouth on a flavor explosion.

Ingredients

To make authentic homemade birria, it is quite simple, but you need both the proper ingredients, as well as patience :).

You will need:

  • Dried Chiles: Aside from the meat, the most necessary component of authentic birria are dried chiles. We will actually need three types of dried chiles:
    Guajillo chiles, ancho chiles and arbol chiles. These are typically found in the international food aisle of your local grocery store.

  • Spices: To grow the flavor, we need a combination of garlic, ground cumin, ground clove, black peppercorn, bay leaves,
    mexican oregano and mexican cinnamon.

  • Vegetables: Aside from the chiles, we need tomatoes, garlic and white onions.

  • Liquids: We need liquid in order for the meat to cook in. We need a good quality beef broth and vinegar.
    You can also use apple cider vinegar as well!

  • Meat: Birria is traditionally made with goat or lamb, but the most popular meat used to make birria is beef chuck roast.
    Other popular types of meat include short ribs, oxtail or beef shanks.

How to Make Birria

Step 1

Get your dutch oven and set it over medium-high heat, with a high smoke-point oil.
Start by searing your meat on all sides until all sides are browned. Once the meat is seared, take it out and set it aside.

Step 2

In a separate medium pot, add all of the chiles and the vegatables, along with your cinnamon,
bay leaves and peppercorn, and cover everything with water. Once that is brought to a boil,
lower the heat, cover it and cook for 10 minutes.

Step 3

Once the chiles are softened from their dried state, transfer everything we just boiled and transfer it to a blender.
Add one cup of the chile water. Add in beef broth, vinegar and other remaining spices.
Blend until the mixture is smooth. This should take about five minutes.

Step 4

Strain the blended sauce into the pot with the seared meat. Stir the meat and bring it to a boil.
Once it is boiling, move to low, cover it and cook the meat for about three to three and-a-half hours or until the meat is falling apart.
Shred the meat and serve!

Step 5

Take your flour tortilla and warm it up a bit. This makes the tortilla more pliable. Add your beans, rice, cheese and authentic birria and roll your burrito up.

Step 6

Finally warm the tortilla up with a pan once again to get it crsipy. Having it crisp will make it the edges stick. Now you have just made an authentic birria burrito!
